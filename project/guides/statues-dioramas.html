Display Case Buying Guide for
Statues, Dioramas, and Centerpieces

A centerpiece deserves a centerpiece case. The piece you waited eighteen months for shouldn't share a shelf with anything. Here's how to size, light, and seal a display worthy of the work.

Measure the silhouette, not the box

Statues are rarely cubic. Sword arms, capes, bases, debris — the silhouette is almost always wider than the shipping carton. Add 8–10cm of clearance to every dimension you measure off the piece itself.

The plinth is part of the case

A statue presented at floor height reads as cargo. Presented at chest height, it reads as art. Plan for a plinth or a riser; the case sits on top.

Front-key, back-fill

The cinematic standard works in display too. A primary light from the front 3/4 angle, a softer fill from behind to separate the piece from the back panel.

Why sealing matters more on statues

Painted resin and polystone attract dust electrostatically. Without a sealed case, you'll be cleaning it weekly — and every cleaning is a small risk to the paintwork.
