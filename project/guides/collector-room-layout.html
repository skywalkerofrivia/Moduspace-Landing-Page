How to Plan a Collector Room
Without Making It Look Cluttered

CategoryRoom Design
Read12 min
PublishedMarch 2026
Paired withAll Moducases

The difference between a collection and chaos is rhythm. Six layout principles, borrowed from gallery design, that work as well in a 200 sq ft room as they do in a hallway.

The grid is your friend

Galleries don't hang work randomly. They establish a baseline — usually eye-height for the centers — and let the work breathe along it. Pick one shared horizontal axis across the room and let every case sit on it.

Negative space is the work

For every case, leave a clear margin equal to at least one-third of the case width. Without that breathing room, the eye reads "storage" instead of "display."

One hero per wall

Multiple hero pieces compete and cancel out. One — usually a centerpiece statue or a UCS build — anchors the wall. Everything else supports.

I sent the team a floor plan with a Sharpie. They sent back a wall layout I would never have thought of.

Light the case, not the room

Ambient room light flattens. Per-case lighting, even at low intensity, defines each piece in its own space. Dim the room. Light the work.

A single case finish across the room

Mixed metals and frame colors fragment the space. One finish — bronze, black, or platinum — across every case unifies a 40-piece collection into a single statement.

When to get help

Rooms are harder than walls. If yours is bigger than 100 sq ft or has more than three sight lines, book a consultation. We've planned several hundred and we don't charge for it.
