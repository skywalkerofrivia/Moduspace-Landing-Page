How to Choose a Display Case
for 1/6 Scale Figures

Hot Toys, Sideshow, Threezero. Every figure in the 12-inch class has its own posing demands — and every collector eventually realizes their case is either two inches too short or four inches too wide. This guide is the one we wish we'd had.

The numbers nobody publishes

A standing 1/6 figure averages 30cm in height. That's the easy part. The hard part: most poseable figures benefit from at least 15cm of headroom for arm-up poses, weapon overhead, or cape spread.

  • Minimum interior height: 45cm
  • Comfortable interior height: 50–55cm
  • Width per single figure: 25cm minimum, 30cm comfortable
  • Depth: 30cm absolute minimum

Where the light falls

Top-down strip lighting flatters most 1/6 figures by emphasizing the sculpted face. Bottom-up uplight is dramatic but flattens detail. Side-mount lighting is best for figures with strong silhouette work.

I measured every figure I owned before ordering. Sixth was the only series that didn't need a custom.

When two become a scene

Multi-figure dioramas in 1/6 need width, not depth. Plan for 50–60cm of clearance between figures so they read as a composition rather than a row.
