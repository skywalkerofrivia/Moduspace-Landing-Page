Display Case Buying Guide
for Statues, Dioramas,
and Centerpiece Pieces.

Statues·10 min read·February 2026·Paired with Max & Max Plus

A statue isn't a figure scaled up. It's a different category of object — heavier, more delicate in finish, often more expensive, and almost always meant to be the centerpiece of a room. The display logic that works for a 1/6 figure breaks down at this scale, and the consequences of getting it wrong are visible from across the room.

The centerpiece problem

Centerpiece pieces — Prime 1 dioramas, Sideshow Premium Format statues, museum-quality reproductions — have a unique display challenge: they have to look right from every viewing angle, from across the room and from a metre away. Most cases optimise for one or the other. The frame disappears at distance but reveals itself uncomfortably close, or vice versa.

The Max and Max Plus series were designed around this dual-distance problem. Wider sightlines, deeper internal volume, and a frame profile that minimises visual interruption from any angle — the result is a case that lets the work be seen properly whether you're walking past or studying it.

How to measure for a statue

The first measurement collectors take is height. The first measurement they should take is diagonal — corner to corner across the base, including any extended limbs, bases, or scenery elements. A 1/4 scale Predator that's 740mm tall might require 920mm of internal length once the spear, the dreadlocks, and the trophy display are accounted for.

The second measurement is base depth, including any extended diorama elements. Statue bases often extend 30–60mm beyond the figure's silhouette, and a case sized to the figure rather than the base will crop the base against the front panel — destroying the proportions of the work as the artist intended.

The third, and most overlooked, is overhead clearance for accent lighting. A directional spotlight installed inside the case needs 80–120mm of clearance above the highest point of the work. Skipping this calculation results in lights that either touch the figure or sit so close that the heat affects the paint application over time.

The lighting question

Statues benefit from directional lighting in ways figures rarely do. The depth of paint application, the modelling of muscle and fabric, the metallic finishes used on weapons and armour — all of these come alive under directional light and look flat under ambient room lighting. The default setup for a Max series case is two adjustable spotlights mounted at 30° from vertical, both warm-temperature.

For dioramas with multiple focal characters, three-point lighting may be appropriate: a key light, a fill, and a back light to separate the figure from the diorama background. This is gallery-grade complexity, and not every collection needs it — but for the truly important pieces, the difference is striking.

Sealing and humidity

Statues and dioramas with painted resin surfaces are surprisingly sensitive to humidity. High humidity over time can soften paint applications. Low humidity can cause certain resin formulations to develop fine crazing across the surface. Most domestic environments fluctuate enough to do gradual damage over years.

A sealed Moducase case provides remarkably stable internal conditions. The magnetic edge seals plus the brush-seal door create an environment that buffers the figure against rapid humidity changes. For collectors in particularly dry or humid climates, an internal humidity card or small silica reservoir extends this stability further.

Conservation note

Resin and polystone statues should not be displayed in direct sunlight under any conditions. UV exposure causes pigment degradation that is irreversible. Even indirect daylight over years will cause visible fading on darker pigments. The Max Plus lighting kits use UV-filtered LEDs as standard.

The Max & Max Plus difference

Max and Max Plus are not larger versions of the smaller Moducase series. They're a different design language — built around the assumption that the work inside is the focal point of the room, not part of a wall. Single-piece presentation. Heavier-grade frame. Reinforced base panel. Higher-output lighting kits with finer dimming control.

The Max series fits most 1/4 scale statues and small to medium dioramas. The Max Plus extends to 1/3 scale work and large dioramas. Both share the connection system with the rest of the Moduspace catalog, so a centerpiece Max Plus can sit at the heart of a wall built from Sixth or DF cases — a room with a clear hierarchy.
